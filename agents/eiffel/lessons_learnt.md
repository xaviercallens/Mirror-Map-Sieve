# Eiffel Lessons Learnt
*   **Lesson 1**: Scoping prompts must have clear domain guards. In earlier runs, lacking domain checks caused the generator to output airline Operations Research flight jargon (like altéa, flights, crew scheduling) for pure mathematical domains.
*   **Lesson 2**: Shifting IP reports from speculative monetization to concrete system-utility benchmarking (e.g. LLM prover training dataset value) creates much more defensible claims.
*   **Lesson 3**: Budget guards must be hardcoded at the pipeline level ($30 limit) to prevent runaway loops in multi-agent discussions.
