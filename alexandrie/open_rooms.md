## Room 1: The Quantum Topologist ($K_n$ Crossing Optimization)
**Focus:** Quantum Circuit Routing & VLSI Superconducting Logic

This room explores the applications of the newly implemented `Runux-Math-Kernel` solver for the Zarankiewicz crossing number conjecture. 
- **The Problem:** Modern quantum computers suffer from decoherence when executing SWAP gates to route distant qubits.
- **The Solution:** By embedding qubit interaction graphs into physical hardware planes utilizing our exact/heuristic $K_n$ solvers, we can find layout embeddings that strictly minimize edge crossings. 
- **Collaboration Goals:** We invite physicists and quantum compiler engineers to utilize the Rust solver to parameterize specific superconducting architectures (e.g., IBM Heavy Hex) and submit PRs with hardware-specific constraints.

## Room 2: The Cryptographic Geometer (Calabi-Yau $c_5$ Periods)
**Focus:** Post-Quantum Lattice Cryptography & Homomorphic Encryption

This room delves into the integration of high-dimensional algebraic geometry with cryptography.
- **The Problem:** As quantum computers threaten RSA and ECC, lattice-based cryptography requires highly complex, unpredictable high-dimensional structures to secure data.
- **The Solution:** We leverage the hypergeometric reductions of $c_5$ period identities (proven in Lean 4 and integrated numerically in Rust) to generate fundamentally new topological lattices.
- **Collaboration Goals:** We invite cryptographers to analyze the "noise budget" boundaries generated by our numerical integrator for Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) schemes, aiming to discover lattices that inherently resist both classical lattice-reduction attacks (LLL) and quantum Grover searches.

## Room 3: The Arithmetic Geometer (The Callens-Schmidt Sequence)
