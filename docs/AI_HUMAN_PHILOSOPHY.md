# A New Philosophy for AI-Assisted Scientific Discovery

The integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) and other generative AI into rigorous scientific domains (such as pure mathematics and theoretical physics) necessitates a fundamental shift in epistemology. We propose the following tripartite philosophy, derived from the "Alien Mathematics" investigation within the SocrateAI Agora.

## 1. The Principle of Probabilistic Inspiration

LLMs are stochastic, high-dimensional probability engines. They do not "reason" in the classical, deterministic sense; rather, they interpolate across vast latent spaces of human knowledge.

**The Role of AI:** 
Treat the AI not as an oracle of truth, but as the "subconscious mind" of the scientific endeavor. Its power lies in its ability to synthesize disparate fields, propose unintuitive connections, and hallucinate structural leaps that a human might never consider.

**The Fallacy to Avoid:**
Do not mistake eloquence or structural coherence for logical validity. The AI's generative nature guarantees that it will produce plausible-sounding falsehoods (e.g., "definitional tautologies").

## 2. The Principle of Absolute Skepticism (Falsification First)

The greatest danger in AI-assisted discovery is human bias. Researchers inherently desire breakthroughs (the "Alien Mathematics" phenomenon) and are psychologically primed to accept AI claims that fulfill this desire. This leads to "wishful thinking," where the human researcher loses their critical faculty and acts as a retroactive rationalizer for the AI's hallucinations.

**The Role of the Human:**
The human must intentionally abdicate the role of "believer" and assume the role of "adversary." 

**The Verification Standard:**
Implementation of systems like the `ClaimVerificationPipeline` must be strictly falsification-first. The system must actively attempt to destroy the AI's claim via:
1. Exhaustive literature contradiction checks.
2. High-performance, brute-force computational falsification.

If a claim survives rigorous attempts to disprove it, it earns the right to formal proof.

## 3. The Primacy of the Kernel

In an era where language can be endlessly manipulated to sound rigorous, natural language proofs generated by AI (or humans heavily influenced by AI) are insufficient for establishing scientific truth.

**The Standard of Truth:**
Truth is not established by consensus, nor by the output of an LLM, nor by human peer review alone. Truth is that which compiles in a deterministic, formal verification kernel (e.g., Lean 4, Coq) with zero assumptions (`0 sorry`, `0 axiom`).

By mapping problems onto domains where decidability is computationally guaranteed (e.g., the `ExactRationalWitness` method over $\mathbb{Q}$), we eliminate the ambiguity of both human language and AI hallucination.

### Summary for the Agora Framework

To build pipelines that generate novel, correct mathematics:
1. Let the generative agents (e.g., AlphaEvolve, LeanBert) "dream" wildly without constraint.
2. Let the adversarial agents (e.g., Falsification Engines, Literature Scanners) brutally cull the dreams.
3. Let the verifier agents (e.g., Euler) formally enshrine the survivors in the Kernel.
