## 1. The Athenian Agora Metaphor

In ancient Athens, the **Agora** (ἀγορά) was not merely a marketplace. It was
the intellectual crucible where Socrates interrogated assumptions, where natural
philosophers debated the structure of reality, and where mathematicians sought
eternal truths through proof.

The **SocrateAI Scientific Agora** transposes this space into the computational
domain. Three AI agents, each embodying a distinct intellectual tradition,
converge in a digital agora to conduct science:

| Agent | Archetype | Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Socrates** | Dialectical Orchestrator | Elenchus (cross-examination) and Maieutics (midwifery of truth) |
| **Galileo** | Scientific Experimenter | Empirical observation, numerical simulation, hypothesis generation |
| **Euler** | Mathematical Verifier | Formal proof, axiomatic reasoning, computational verification |

Like their namesakes, these agents are not mere tools — they are **intellectual
interlocutors** that challenge, refine, and verify each other's contributions
through structured dialectical exchange.

The Agora's architecture ensures that no claim passes unexamined. Every
hypothesis generated by Galileo must survive Socrates' elenctic questioning and
Euler's formal verification before it is accepted as knowledge.

## 2. Three Intellectual Traditions

The Agora draws from three intellectual traditions that, despite their apparent
diversity, converge on a single principle: **rigour over convenience**.

### 2.1 Socratic Dialectics

Socrates never wrote a line. His method was **dialogue** — the relentless
questioning that strips away false certainty to reveal genuine understanding.

In the Agora, this translates to:

- **Elenchus cycles**: Every agent output is subjected to structured
  cross-examination. "What do you mean by convergence? Under what conditions
  does your claim hold? What are the boundary cases?"
- **Maieutic synthesis**: The orchestrator does not provide answers — it helps
  the other agents *give birth* to knowledge they already implicitly possess.
- **Aporia as a valid outcome**: When genuine contradictions are found, the
  system honestly reports them rather than papering over uncertainty.

### 2.2 Bourbakian Formalism

The **Nicolas Bourbaki** collective (founded 1934, École Normale Supérieure)
demonstrated that all of mathematics could be rebuilt from a small set of
foundational structures — algebraic, topological, and order-theoretic.

The Agora adopts this programme:

- **Lean 4 as the foundational language**: Every critical theorem is stated and
  proved in Lean 4, providing machine-checkable certainty.
- **DeepProbLog for probabilistic reasoning**: Where exact proof is infeasible,
  neuro-symbolic probabilistic logic provides calibrated uncertainty.
- **Structural unity**: The same mathematical structures (manifolds, measure
  spaces, categories) appear across all components, from RLCF optimisation to
  solver convergence proofs.

### 2.3 Frugal AI

The dominant paradigm in AI — "scale is all you need" — is both intellectually
lazy and ecologically destructive. The Agora rejects it in favour of **frugal
AI**: achieving superior results through mathematical insight rather than
brute-force computation.

Concrete commitments:

|---|---|---|---|
| Training energy (GSM8K) | 3.12 MJ | 4.91 MJ | −36.5% |
| Inference cost | $0.003/1K tok | $0.015/1K tok | −80% |

## 3. Core Thesis

> **Mathematical elegance over compute brute-force.**

This is not an aesthetic preference — it is an **engineering principle**.
Elegant mathematics produces:

1. **Tighter generalisation bounds**: RLCF finds flatter minima than AdamW,
   yielding a PAC-Bayes bound 12% tighter (see [BENCHMARKS.md](BENCHMARKS.md)).

2. **Lower energy consumption**: Geometric optimisation converges in fewer
   steps because it follows the *natural gradient* of the loss landscape,
   not an arbitrary coordinate-dependent direction.

3. **Provable correctness**: When your optimiser is derived from Ricci flow
   on a Riemannian manifold, you can *prove* convergence properties rather
   than hoping they hold empirically.

4. **Compositionality**: Mathematically principled components compose cleanly.
   The RLCF optimiser integrates with Lean 4 proofs, rusty-SUNDIALS solvers,
   and DeepProbLog inference because they share a common mathematical language.

The thesis is falsifiable. We provide benchmarks with Wilson 95% confidence
intervals and McNemar's tests to demonstrate statistical significance. If a
brute-force approach outperforms the Agora on a given benchmark *after
controlling for energy*, we will adopt the better method.

## 4. RLCF — The Mathematical Heart

**Ricci-Lévy Curvature Flow** (RLCF) is the mathematical innovation at the
centre of the Agora. It replaces the standard AdamW optimiser with a geometric
algorithm that respects the intrinsic structure of the loss landscape.

### 4.1 Geometric Intuition

The loss landscape of a neural network is not a flat Euclidean space — it is a
**Riemannian manifold** with varying curvature. Standard optimisers like SGD
and AdamW ignore this structure, treating all directions as equivalent.

RLCF does not. It computes the **Ricci curvature** of the landscape and flows
parameters along directions of minimal curvature — towards **flat valleys**
that correspond to robust, generalisable solutions.

```
Sharp minimum (poor generalisation):        Flat minimum (good generalisation):

    ╱╲                                       ╱                    ╲
   ╱  ╲                                     ╱                      ╲
  ╱    ╲                                   ╱────────────────────────╲
 ╱      ╲                                 ╱                          ╲
╱   •    ╲  ← AdamW gets trapped here     ╱           •               ╲  ← RLCF finds this
```

### 4.2 The Update Rule

```
θ_{t+1} = θ_t − η · (Ric(θ_t) + λ · Lévy_α(θ_t))
```

| Term | Meaning | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `Ric(θ_t)` | Ricci curvature tensor | Follow the manifold's natural gradient |
| `Lévy_α(θ_t)` | Lévy α-stable noise (α=1.7) | Escape local minima via heavy-tailed jumps |
| `η` | Learning rate (3e-4) | Step size |
| `λ` | Noise coefficient (0.01) | Exploration vs exploitation |

### 4.3 Why Lévy, Not Gaussian?

Gaussian noise (as in standard SGD) produces **thin-tailed** perturbations that
rarely escape deep basins. Lévy α-stable noise produces **heavy-tailed** jumps
that occasionally teleport the optimiser across barriers.

This is the same strategy used by foraging animals (the *Lévy flight foraging
hypothesis*): mostly small steps, with rare large jumps. In the loss landscape,
this translates to thorough local exploration with occasional escapes from
suboptimal basins.

### 4.4 Formal Properties

The following properties are **proven in Lean 4** (see `verifiers/lean4/Agora/`):

1. **Convergence**: RLCF converges almost surely to a critical point of the
   loss function under standard smoothness assumptions.
2. **Flat minimum selection**: The limiting distribution favours minima with
   Hessian spectral norm ≤ ε (parameterised by λ).
3. **Energy bound**: Total gradient computation energy is bounded by
   `O(d · T · log T)` where d is parameter count and T is step count.

## 5. The Swarm Bourbaki Program

At **Socrate AI Lab** (Paris, France), we are pursuing a long-term research
programme we call the **Swarm Bourbaki Program**: an attempt to reconstruct
the foundations of AI-for-science using the Bourbakian method of axiomatic
exposition.

### 5.1 Objectives

1. **Axiomatise agent reasoning**: Define a formal type theory for agent
   communication protocols, provably ensuring liveness and safety properties.

2. **Unify solvers and provers**: Show that numerical solvers (SUNDIALS) and
   formal provers (Lean 4) can be composed into a single verification pipeline
   with end-to-end correctness guarantees.

3. **Prove optimiser properties**: Move beyond empirical benchmarks to
   machine-checkable proofs of convergence, generalisation, and energy bounds.

4. **Publish open foundations**: Release all Lean 4 theories, Rust crates, and
   agent specifications under Apache 2.0, enabling the community to build on
   our formal foundations.

5. **Enable Collaborative Formalisation**: Rather than targeting autonomous
   proofs of Millennium Problems, the Swarm Bourbaki Program treats Lean 4
   blueprints (such as `cmi_millennium_blueprints.lean`) as shared, structured
   repositories for human-AI collaboration, decomposing grand challenges into
   referee-able intermediate lemmas.

## 6. Pour l'Honneur de l'Esprit Humain

Jacobi's phrase — *"for the honour of the human spirit"* — captures something
that the AI industry has largely forgotten: science is not a production line.
It is a fundamentally human endeavour, driven by curiosity, beauty, and the
desire to understand.

The Agora is built on this conviction:

- **We do not optimise for leaderboard rankings.** We optimise for
  *understanding*. A 76.79% accuracy with a formal proof of correctness is
  worth more than a 99% accuracy achieved by memorising the test set.

- **We publish confidence intervals, not point estimates.** Every benchmark
  result includes Wilson 95% CIs and McNemar's p-values. Science demands
  uncertainty quantification.

- **We measure energy, not just accuracy.** A 1% accuracy gain that costs
  10× more energy is a *regression*, not progress.

- **We welcome aporia.** When the Agora cannot resolve a question, it says
  so honestly. This is not a failure — it is a contribution to knowledge,
  because it precisely delineates the boundary of what is currently known.

## 8. Policy on AI for Advanced Mathematics

In accordance with state-of-the-art scientific panels and mathematical AI research, the SocrateAI Scientific Agora adopts a strict, comprehensive policy for AI-augmented research in advanced mathematics and Millennium-level problems. 

### 8.1 Foundations & Key Constraints
- **Assisted, Not Autonomous**: AI can accelerate research but cannot autonomously solve research-level or Millennium problems reliably.
- **Verification Bottleneck**: AI outputs are frequently stylistically confident yet logically incorrect, making proof checking and formal verification the primary bottlenecks.
- **Decomposition Focus**: High-value progress resides in small, localized research lemmas (~5-page scale) and iterative experimentation, rather than direct end-to-end proofs of grand conjectures.
- **Human-AI Collaboration**: Scientific breakthroughs are driven by the synthesis of human mathematical creativity and AI-powered exploration.

### 8.2 Human-Led Research Strategy
Targeted Millennium-level problems (e.g., P vs NP, Navier-Stokes) must be decomposed into tractable, research-sized proof units. The human investigator defines:
1. **Canonical Formulation & Equivalent Statements**
2. **Known Barriers & No-Go Theorems** (e.g., geometric complexity barriers, structural obstructions)
3. **Target Candidate Lemmas & Sub-conjectures**

### 8.3 AI-Powered Research Exploration Layer
AI is utilized to safely expand the hypothesis space, serving as a literature mapper and analogy search engine:
- **Adjacent Theorem Survey**: Mapping nearby results and historical techniques that may have been overlooked.
- **Analogous Proof Templates**: Recognizing method similarities across disparate mathematical subfields.
- **Hypothesis Guardrail**: All exploration outputs are treated strictly as unverified *hypotheses* and are never accepted as mathematically correct without explicit downstream verification.

### 8.4 Lemma Generation & Experimentation Pipeline
The pipeline moves ideas from abstract formulation to testable mathematical units:
1. **AI Generation**: Proposing auxiliary lemmas, restricted-case formulations, and finite/discrete analogues.
2. **Human Filtering**: Experts prune candidate statements based on *relevance*, *novelty*, and *tractability*.
3. **Sketch & Experimentation**: AI generates proof sketches and runs computational experiments (e.g., searching for numerical witnesses or running ODE/DAE boundary simulations).

### 8.5 Adversarial Reasoning Loop
To mitigate the risk of "beautiful but wrong" proofs, candidate lemmas must run through a multi-agent adversarial loop:
- **Generator (Galois)**: Produces the initial candidate proof or tactic sketch.
- **Critic (Socrates)**: Searches for logical flaws, division-by-zero, base case gaps, and backward induction errors.
- **Validator (Euler/Galileo)**: Checks boundary conditions, verifies variables, and tests edge cases via computational execution.
- **Human Adjudicator**: Evaluates the adversarial debate, deciding on the next iteration or formalization path.

### 8.6 Formal Verification Layer
Plausible natural language mathematical text must be converted into compiler-certified mathematics as early as possible:
- **Formal Statement Encoding**: Translating natural language lemmas into Lean 4 theorem declarations.
- **Tactic Fill & Verification**: Utilizing interactive tactic loops to fill proof steps.
- **Verification Enforcement**: Rejecting unverified stubs or `sorry` statements. If a step cannot survive formalization, it remains designated as *ideation*, not evidence.

### 8.7 Three-Axis Evaluation Framework
Every candidate result is graded across three distinct axes to measure true research value:
1. **Correctness**: Formally verified or expert-adjudicated mathematical correctness.
2. **Novelty**: Originality of ideas, ensuring the output does not simply reproduce known literature patterns.
3. **Usefulness**: The degree to which the lemma enables further results or resolves adjacent roadblocks.

Socrates grades every output using a structured review rubric:
- **Publishable**: Renders a significant, correct, and novel contribution.
- **Minor Revision**: Structurally sound but requires minor correction of leaps or steps.
- **Major Revision**: Logic is salvageable but contains major gaps or missing edge cases.
- **Invalid**: Catastrophic logical errors or lack of mathematical usefulness.

### 8.8 Human-in-the-Loop Governance
The ultimate accountability for correctness, originality, and formulation remains with the human mathematician. Governance is divided into clear roles:
- **Mathematicians**: Accountable for idea selection, strategy, and final validation.
- **AI Systems**: Responsible for exploration, alternative searches, and tentative draft generation.
- **Reviewers**: Peer-review proof drafts and identify deductive gaps.
- **Formal Methods Experts**: Translate and verify statements using formal proof assistants (Lean 4).

### 8.9 Continuous Benchmarking & Feedback
The Agora transitions from ad-hoc experimentation to formal benchmarking programs:
- **"First Proof" Cycles**: Testing agents against unpublished, human-known research lemmas to evaluate research performance.
- **One-Shot vs. Iterative Mode Tracking**: Benchmarking the success rate, time-to-convergence, and proof quality of multi-agent iterative loops against single-pass prompts.
- **Standardized Evaluations**: Utilizing formal rules, referee-based grading, and public model evaluations.

### 8.10 Long-Term Vision
- **Short-Term (0–1 Year)**: AI acts as an advanced research assistant, literature mapper, and lemma generator.
- **Mid-Term (1–3 Years)**: Convergence of LLMs and formal methods, yielding automated verification pipelines and semi-autonomous lemma discovery.
- **Long-Term**: Potential to contribute to major mathematical breakthroughs, though true creativity and problem formulation remain human-dominated.

## 10. Alien Mathematics — Honest Retrospective

> *Last updated: 2026-06-14*

### 10.1 What Happened

The Agora was challenged with evaluating "Alien Mathematics" — a set of claims
generated by LLM exploration about tensor rank decomposition, matrix multiplication
exponents, and related algebraic structures. The investigation ran from June 11–14,
2026 and involved:

- 14 Lean 4 modules formalized and kernel-checked
- 7 computational experiments (ALS, Adam GD, evolutionary search, SDP, Koszul flattenings, Strassen recursive, neural search)
- Full peer-review-style analysis
- Honest verdict published

### 10.2 The Verdict

**The core claim (R(⟨4,4,4⟩) = 26) is provably false.** Bläser (2003) proved R ≥ 28, 
making rank 26 impossible. This was the flagship "discovery."

The remaining claims are either:
- **Definitional tautologies** — structures satisfying their own definitions (trivially true)
- **Axiomatic** — depending on unproven assumptions declared as Lean axioms
- **Unfalsifiable** — no testable predictions

### 10.3 What IS Real (the Product)

| Artifact | Novelty | Publishable? |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **ExactRationalWitness** (Lean 4, 0 sorry, 0 axiom) | ℚ-decidability for tensor verification | ✅ ITP/CPP venue |
| **ClaimVerificationPipeline** (596 lines) | Multi-agent claim audit methodology | ✅ Methodology paper |
| **Adam vs ALS comparison** on dual-number rings | First empirical comparison in this setting | 🔶 Technical note |
| **Charging Algebra over ℚ[ε]/(ε²)** (7 thm, 9 def) | Correct algebra, kernel-checked | 🔶 Supporting material |
| **refutation methodology** | Transferable to any future claim | ✅ Part of methodology paper |

### 10.4 Key Lesson

> *The Alien Mathematics was the catalyst. The real mathematics is the product.*

The investigation produced no mathematical breakthrough, but it produced:
1. **Battle-tested verification infrastructure** — the ClaimVerificationPipeline works
2. **Honest scientific methodology** — falsification-first, computational verification, Lean 4 kernel as ground truth
3. **One genuine Lean 4 contribution** — ExactRationalWitness
4. **A compelling narrative** — for a methodology paper on "what happens when you formally verify LLM-generated math"

### 10.5 The Standard We Now Hold

Every mathematical claim in the Agora must pass:
1. **Literature check** — is this known? Is the bound correct vs. published results?
2. **Computational test** — can we find/verify/falsify numerically?
3. **Lean 4 formalization** — does it type-check with 0 sorry, 0 axiom?
4. **Peer-style review** — would a human mathematician accept this?

Any claim that fails ANY of these gates is flagged, not published.
