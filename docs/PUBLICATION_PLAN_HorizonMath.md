# Publication Plan: HorizonMath Benchmark Integration

**Target:** `https://github.com/ewang26/HorizonMath`
**Authors:** Xavier Callens, Socrate AI Lab Team, HorizonMath Core Contributors
**Proposed Title:** *Extrapolative AI for Open Mathematical Problems: A Test-Time Compute Architecture Outperforming Frontier LLMs on Solvability Class 3*

## Objective
To publish a formal scientific paper based on the SymBrain v11 achievements on the HorizonMath benchmark (specifically the Top 5 Solvability Class 3 problems) and merge the findings upstream into the HorizonMath repository as a new state-of-the-art (SOTA) baseline.

## Phase 1: Artifact & Data Preparation (Days 1-3)
1. **Compile the LaTeX Monograph:** Finalize the `.tex` and `.pdf` files generated by the Agora framework during the Top 5 Strict Mode execution. Ensure Lean 4 verification logs are attached as supplementary material.
2. **Sanitize Telemetry Data:** Extract the `pynvml` power consumption logs, MCTS depth metrics, and GCP billing footprints to prove the frugality constraint (<$100, actual $4.76).
3. **Format the Evaluation Report:** Use the `EVALUATION_REPORT_v11.md` as the core of the "Results and Discussion" section, highlighting Epistemological Integrity, Search Architecture (MCTS vs Parametric Memory), and Swarm Dynamics.

## Phase 2: Paper Drafting & arXiv Submission (Days 4-7)
1. **Abstract & Introduction:** Frame the failure of autoregressive models on Solvability Class 3 and introduce the Socratic/Galois neuro-symbolic approach. Cite *Wang et al. (2026)* heavily.
2. **Methodology:** Describe the Lévy-flight MCTS, Lean 4 bounding, Null-Payload Circuit Breaker, and Anti-Echo Swarm protocols.
3. **Results:** Detail the Triangular SAW ($\pi \cdot e^{1/e}$) and Square SAW dimensional shift heuristics. Emphasize the verifiable error bounding.
4. **Submission:** Submit to arXiv under `math.HO` (History and Overview) and `cs.AI` (Artificial Intelligence).

## Phase 3: Upstream Open-Source Integration (Days 8-10)
1. **Fork the Repository:** Create a fork of `ewang26/HorizonMath`.
2. **Add Leaderboard Entry:** Update the `data/baselines.json` and `README.md` in the fork to include SymBrain v11's performance, making sure to categorize it under a new "Test-Time Compute + Formal Verification" category rather than standard LLMs.
3. **Add Case Studies:** Include the generated `symbrain_v11_horizonmath_top5_strict.pdf` in a new `evaluations/symbrain/` folder in the HorizonMath repo.
4. **Pull Request:** Open a comprehensive PR against `main` on the HorizonMath repository titled: `[SOTA Update] Add SymBrain v11 MCTS Evaluation for Solvability Class 3`. Include the link to the arXiv preprint.

## Phase 4: Peer Review & Community Outreach
- Announce the findings on academic Twitter, highlighting the difference between "interpolative" standard LLMs and "extrapolative" MCTS models.
- Reach out directly to Erik Y. Wang and the Oxford/Harvard HorizonMath team to present the findings, suggesting future collaboration for the upcoming SymBrain v12 PSLQ integration.
